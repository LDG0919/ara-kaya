Data Deletion

App: Star catcher - 9-language Korean-centered vocabulary
Last updated: May 10, 2026

Star catcher does not store any user data on external servers. All data is kept on your device only, so uninstalling the App removes it. This page explains where your data lives and how to delete it.

1. What Data Is Stored, and Where

All user data in Star catcher is stored locally on your device only:

  • Bookmarked words
  • Hide-known-words list
  • Custom entries you've added
  • Quiz and learning progress
  • Preferences such as background image, colors, and text size
  • Backup files (.json) you've exported

Nothing is transmitted to or stored on our servers.

2. How to Delete All Data

The simplest way is to uninstall the App. Removing the App also removes all local data listed above.

  1. Long-press the Star catcher icon on your home screen or app drawer.
  2. Select "Uninstall" (or App info → Uninstall).
  3. Confirm. The App and all local data are removed together.

3. Reset Data Without Uninstalling

If you want to keep the App but clear stored data:

  1. Open Android Settings → Apps → Star catcher.
  2. Select "Storage" or "Storage & cache".
  3. Tap "Clear data". The App returns to its first-install state.

You can also clear bookmarks, custom entries, learning history, and other items individually from within the App's settings.

4. Deleting Backup Files (.json)

If you've exported a .json backup using the in-app backup feature, that file is stored in your device's Downloads folder or wherever you saved it. You'll need to delete it manually using your file manager.

5. Resetting Your Advertising Identifier

Star catcher does not identify you personally, but it uses Google AdMob to show ads. The advertising ID (AAID) used by AdMob can be reset or disabled in your device settings:

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization"

6. In-App Purchase (Remove Ads)

Records of the remove-ads in-app purchase are kept by your Google Play account. Star catcher does not manage purchase records itself; they are governed by Google Play's data policies.
